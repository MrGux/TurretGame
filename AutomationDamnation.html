Introduction

        Automation Damnation is a new take on the classic PvP card game playable with up to four players but works best with two. Automation Damnation is designed to speed up the rate of play of a typical card game by forcing players to play strictly on the offensive. By eliminating the typical attack/defense structure, Automation Damnation takes players out of their comfort zones to force them into coming up with creative combos instead of relying on predictable patterns that have become common place in modern card games.

Order of Play

        Turns in Automation Damnation happen over 3 phases. The first is the play phase. One at a time players take a turn deploying any turret cards they would like to play from their hands.

        After the play phase is the building phase. During this phase all turrets gain a build level.

        The building phase is proceeded by the attack phase. In the attack phase, all fully built and loaded turrets automatically fire.

Card Types

        In Automation Damnation there are three card types: Automoton cards, Turret cards, and Engineer cards.

        In Automation Damnation players each have their own Automoton. A player's Automoton is a representation of themselves and are automatically played in a stack at the start of the game, removing the top card when a stage is defeated.

        Turret and Engineer cards are held in the players hand. Each player intitially draws 5 cards to start the game and 1 more at the start of each of their turns.

        Turret cards are the units of Automation Damnation. They come in 4 varieties and each have unique properties. All turrets have a building level requirement, varying by the power level of the turret. This building level requirement is a value from 1 to 3. For each level of building required, a turret is turned 45 degrees counter clockwise. Whenever a turret gains a build level the turret card is turned 45 degrees clockwise until it is upright. Once upright a turret is fully built, but still must load, forcing it to skip the next attack phase.

        The final card type is the engineer card. Engineer cards are used by players during the building phase in the same order as the play phase of that turn. Engineer cards effect the game in a variety of ways in relation to build level and damage output of turrets, as well as interacting with other engineer cards. Each player can only play a maximum of 1 engineer card per build phase.

Automoton Cards

        Each players Automoton initially has 7 defense, meaning if an opposing player manages to deal 5 or more damage to the Automoton, it is damaged. Each Automoton can only take damage 3 times before being destroyed. Each player has 3 Automoton cards, each representing a new stage, after taking damage a player's Automoton enters a new stage, going from Strong to Damaged to Critical and then is finally destroyed. The stages of the Automoton have different durability values as well, starting with 7 for the Strong stage, 10 for the Damaged stage, and 15 for the Critical stage.

Turret Cards

        Turret cards exist in 4 varieties.

        The first of turret card is the Basic. This turret requires 1 building level, does 1 damage, and has 1 durability.

        The second turret is the Double Shot. This turret requires 1 building level and does 2 damage but must load again after each round that it fires. This effectively means the turret fires once every other turn. This turret has 2 durability.

        The third turret is the Burst. This turret requires 2 building levels but does 3 damage when it fires! Like the Double Shot, the Burst must load after every shot. This turret has 3 durability.

        The final turret is the Flame Turret. This turret requires 3 building level and deals 2 damage but must be loaded. However, unlike other turrets, this turret does not deal its damage to only one target, and instead deals its damage to all turrets and the Automoton of the player being targeted. This turret has 2 durabilty.

Engineer Cards

        Engineer cards also exist in 4 varieties.

        The first engineer card is the Barricade card. This engineer card allows a player to build a 5 durability wall in front of their turrets. This wall lasts for 1 turn only and is used when an attack is anticipated to protect your turrets and Automoton from taking damage.

        The second card is the Builder Strike card. This card allows players to stop building of turrets for all players including themselves. Players may use this to delay opponents and draw more cards for a combo.

        The third engineer card is the Overdrive! card. This card doubles to damage output of all of your turrets. Players use this card to deal maximum damage to destroy enemy turrets or break through the stages of Automoton defenses.

        Counter is the 4th and final engineer card. This card nullifies the effects of all engineer cards for 1 turn. Counter is the only engineer card that can be played out of turn during the building phase, meaning you can wait until your opponent plays their own engineer card to use it, but still cannot be played if the player has already played an engineer card that turn.

Rules

Turn Order: Players may decide who goes first via coin toss, rock-paper-scissors, or any other method of their choosing, the winner getting to decide who goes first. After this first turn, the player to go first during the play phase moves over once clockwise, much like blinds in Texas Hold'em.

Targeting Players: Players dictate which player their turrets attack at the start of the phase by playing a player token face down. All players then reveal their tokens and attacking commences.

Targeting Units: Once a player has decided which opponent to attack, they decide whether their attacks hit the players turrets or their Automoton. When a player chooses to attack turrets their attacks hit turrets from the target player's left to right. All leftover damage is wasted and does not overflow to the player's Automoton.
